The MIDS LL.M. programme in International Dispute Settlement celebrated the graduation of its 2024-2025 cohort - a diverse group of future leaders in international arbitration. This milestone marks the beginning of their journey into a global alumni network committed to shaping the future of dispute resolution.

Friday, the 19th of September, 2025, marked a momentous occasion as we proudly celebrated the
graduation of the 2024-2025 cohort of the LL.M. in International Dispute
Settlement (MIDS). This remarkable group of students, hailing from diverse
corners of the globe, completed an intense and enriching academic journey - and
are now poised to shape the future of international dispute resolution.

The graduation
ceremony was a fitting culmination of a year defined by rigorous scholarship,
dynamic exchange, and personal growth. We were honored to welcome a
distinguished lineup of speakers who shared their insights and words of
encouragement with our graduates:


Dr Wolfgang Peter (Founding Partner, Peter
& Kim SA)
Prof. Pierre Tercier
Prof. Gabrielle Kaufmann-Kohler
Professor Zachary Douglas KC (MIDS and
CIDS Director; Professor, IHEID)
Professor Nico Krisch (Head of the
International Law Department, IHEID)

Their presence and
contributions added depth and inspiration to the celebration, reminding us of
the enduring impact of the MIDS community.

E. ROSET
Spotlight: Dr. Wolfgang Peter’s keynote address

Dr. Wolfgang Peter,
Founding Partner at Peter & Kim SA, delivered an insightful keynote speech during
the MIDS 2024-2025 Graduation Ceremony.

In his remarks, titled
"Reflections on Changes to the Practice of Arbitration," Dr. Peter
offered a compelling perspective on the evolving landscape of international
arbitration. Drawing on decades of experience at the forefront of the field, he
explored how shifts in practice, technology, and global dynamics are reshaping
the way disputes are resolved across borders.

Whether you're a
student, pursuing a career in investment arbitration or international
commercial arbitration programmes, practitioner, or academic, this
thought-provoking address is a valuable contribution to the ongoing dialogue on
the future of dispute resolution.

To our newest alumni:
welcome to a global network of professionals, scholars, and leaders committed
to excellence in international dispute settlement. The MIDS alumni community
continues to grow stronger each year, and we are thrilled to see how you will
carry forward its legacy.

Congratulations once
again, Class of 2024-2025 - your journey is just beginning.

Friday, the 19th of September, 2025, marked a momentous occasion as we proudly celebrated the graduation of the 2024-2025 cohort of the LL.M. in International Dispute Settlement (MIDS). This remarkable group of students, hailing from diverse corners of the globe, completed an intense and enriching academic journey - and are now poised to shape the future of international dispute resolution. The graduation ceremony was a fitting culmination of a year defined by rigorous scholarship, dynamic exchange, and personal growth. We were honored to welcome a distinguished lineup of speakers who shared their insights and words of encouragement with our graduates:

  • Dr Wolfgang Peter (Founding Partner, Peter & Kim SA)
  • Prof. Pierre Tercier
  • Prof. Gabrielle Kaufmann-Kohler
  • Professor Zachary Douglas KC (MIDS and CIDS Director; Professor, IHEID)
  • Professor Nico Krisch (Head of the International Law Department, IHEID)

Their presence and contributions added depth and inspiration to the celebration, reminding us of the enduring impact of the MIDS community.
