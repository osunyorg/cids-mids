At the heart of Europe, Geneva has been at the center of international relations for more than a century. A historical birthplace of modern international law, and today a hub of expertise with hundreds of international organizations, NGOs, and law firms, Geneva is also in the top 5 worldwide of preferred seats of arbitration, alongside London, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Paris.

With its purposefully niche focus and its original practice-oriented curriculum, the MIDS is an unusual rival to other LL.M. programs at the world's most prestigious academic institutions.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Practice-oriented description: >-

From the course curriculum to the study trips, career services, networking opportunities, and internship options, everything at the MIDS is designed to prepare students for a successful legal career. 

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- An unparalleled community description: >-

Despite being a young academic program, with its faculty, partners, and alumni, the MIDS has constituted in just a decade one of the world's largest and most tight-knit communities of international dispute settlement lawyers.

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: persons title: >- Learn from world-leading experts slug: >- learn-from-world-leading-experts ranks: self: 3 children: 4 data: description: >-

The MIDS is taught by a combination of permanent Geneva-based faculty as well as visiting faculty from top institutions. It includes some of the world's most renowned experts and practitioners in the field of international dispute settlement, ensuring that students get the best, most up-to-date, and practice-relevant training.

When it was created, the MIDS was a bold attempt by academics and practitioners to create an innovative program meant to cater to the specific needs of legal practice in international dispute settlement. 15 years on, it's become an incredible success story, a model in the field, and created an unparalleled global community.
