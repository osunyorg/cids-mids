Being an international student is the promise of an exciting and enriching experience, but also a stressful one. You help you plan ahead so as to make the most of your time in Geneva.

Moving to Switzerland as an international student is an exciting and enriching experience. You will find yourself in a country known for its high quality of life, stunning landscapes, and vibrant cultural scene.

However, whether you just graduated from a previous program or are taking a break from your legal career, the experience can also be unsettling and stressful.

Finding yourself outside your comfort zone, without your usual support networks, and faced with a whole new environment, culture, and language, can be overwhelming at first.

The good news, however, is that you will not be alone: your classmates and you will be in the same boat, and the MIDS team will be there to support and accompany you every step of the way. In a city where half of the population is made out of foreigners, you can also be sure to quickly find helpful resources and new support networks.

In any event, we encourage you not to underestimate what is involved when you decide to join us, and the key to making the transition as smooth as possible is preparation. Below are some main tips:

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Prepare yourself mentally slug: >- prepare-yourself-mentally ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: features title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false children: 3 data: description: >-

A lot of the preparation involved is about getting yourself mentally ready to move to Switzerland: think of all the exciting parts, but make sure you also prepare for all the possible challenges or mishaps. 

elements: - title: >- Educate yourself about life in Switzerland description: >-

In addition to the resources you can find on our website and the informations we will provide you with ahead of time, there is plenty of information you can find online or by asking friends or MIDS alumni and that will help you better understand what to expect and how to prepare.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Manage your expectations description: >-

Whether you are excited or anxious about moving to Geneva, make sure you manage your expectations one way or the other: do not be overly anxious or overly confident.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Understand the cost of living description: >-

Switzerland is known for its high cost of living. Though on par with many other large international cities or university towns, life in Geneva and Switzerland is experience. Budgeting carefully and realistically ahead of time can help the experience feel less frustrating and more enjoyable.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Prepare for unexpected challenges description: >-

Another key is to prepare yourself for potential challenges. Whether it is about your travel plans, your accommodation, and your love ones, many unexpected things can happen by the time you start the MIDS. Prepare yourself mentally and practically for possible challenges, to be equipped to handle them appropriately.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Learn basic French description: >-

While the MIDS is entirely in English and you can easily get by in Geneva without speaking a word of French, getting some basic knowledge can be incredibly helpful for daily interactions and to make your new environement more familiar and less intimidating.

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Get ready for the paperwork slug: >- get-ready-for-the-paperwork ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: features title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false children: 3 data: description: >-

As is always the case when moving somewhere new, moving to Switzerland as an international student will almost inevitably involve quite a bit of paperwork and administrative steps. The more organized and prepared you are, the easier it will feel.

elements: - title: >- Stay organized description: >-

The range of steps you will need to take, forms you will need to fill out, and documents you will need to provide can easily become confusing and overwhelming. Make sure you organize yourself as early as possible to keep track of all the procedures and documents you need.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Pay attention to deadlines description: >-

Keeping track of the documents and procedures is one thing, but you will usually need to complete specific steps at specific times or within specific windows. It is easy to miss deadlines, but it can be very difficult to fix things afterwards. So make sure you are aware and keeping track of deadlines you need to comply with.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Do not procrastinate description: >-

If you are admitted, we will get in touch with you and ask you all sorts of information and provide instructions on next steps. Make sure you respond or complete these tasks in a timely manner. This will make it easier for you and everybody else involved (especially our team).

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Think of all the documents you may need description: >-

Whether when you are preparing to move or after you have moved, bear in mind that you might need all sorts of documents that you do not necessarily often carry with you. Diplomas, birth certificates, attestation of residency, proof of your financial situations, you may be asked all sorts of documents to complete administrative procedures. Start gather these documents proactively and make sure you pack all the ones you need before you travel to Geneva.

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Settle down and enjoy slug: >- settle-down-and-enjoy ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: features title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false children: 3 data: description: >-

You will have a lot to do before and after you move to Geneva, but there will also be a lot to enjoy. 

elements: - title: >- Plan things to look forward to description: >-

As you prepare to move to Geneva for your studies, start planning activities and experiences that you can look forward to. Whether it’s weekend trips to the Alps, attending local festivals, or exploring the cultural offerings of Geneva, having these plans can keep you motivated and excited about your new adventure.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Allow yourself time to rest description: >-

Balancing the excitement of moving with the demands of wrapping up your work or studies at home is crucial. Make sure to schedule regular breaks and downtime to rest and recharge throughout the process of preparing for the move, and after you have moved. Once you are here, this could be as simple as taking a walk along Lake Geneva, practicing physical activities, or enjoying some quiet time aside from all the activities we will have planned for you. Prioritizing rest will help you maintain your energy and focus.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Give yourself enough buffer time description: >-

When planning your move and your schedule, always allow for buffer time between commitments. We especially encourage you to come to Geneva sufficiently ahead of the start of the academic year. This extra time can be invaluable for handling unexpected delays, settling into your new home, or simply taking a moment to breathe. Buffer time helps reduce stress and ensures you can manage your responsibilities without feeling overwhelmed.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Connect with your classmates description: >-

Building connections with your future classmates is an essential part of your graduate experience. Engage in online forums, attend virtual meetups, and participate in pre-arrival events. These interactions not only enhance your learning but also create a support network that can be invaluable once you arrive in Geneva.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Explore and enjoy before things get busy description: >-

Take advantage of the initial weeks before your academic schedule becomes intense to explore and enjoy your new surroundings. Visit local attractions, try new foods, and immerse yourself in the local culture. This exploration will help you feel more at home and provide a solid foundation for when your studies become more demanding.

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: accent_background text: >-

Now you know how to prepare, so get started as soon as you hear from us on your admission, embrace this new step in your life and your career, and be ready for adventures and making the most of your time in beautiful Switzerland!
