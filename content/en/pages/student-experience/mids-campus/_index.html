Studying at the heart of Geneva, our students enjoy state-of-the-art teaching facilities with Lake Geneva and the Alps as a backdrop.

header_text: >- header_cta: display: false label: >- target: "" external: false contents_reading_time: seconds: 228 text: >- 4 minutes contents: - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

The MIDS is headquartered in Villa Moynier, a historic villa on the shores of Lake Geneva with a view to the Mont Blanc. However, as a joint program of the University of Geneva (UNIGE) and the Geneva Graduate Institute (IHEID), MIDS activities take place at both institutions, in the Painpalais area--Geneva's student district near the Old Town--for UNIGE, and at the House of Peace--Maison de la Paix in French--in the International Organizations district for the Graduate Institute.

The joint nature of the program also means that MIDS students can take full advantage of the facilities of both institutions, such as their various libraries, student residences, or sports facilities.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: image title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: text: >-

The facilities used by the MIDS spread across the city but are most concentrated in the international district as part of the "Peace Campus", with most buildings a few minutes away from most of the International Organizations headquartered in Geneva.

Villa Moynier is the shared home of the MIDS and of the Geneva Academy of International Humanitarian Law and Human Rights, another joint center of UNIGE and of the Graduate Institute. While the Villa is home primarily to the MIDS offices, students often attend a handful of classes, workshops, or public events in its main room named after the late international lawyer Antonio Cassese.

notes: >-

Villa Moynier was built in 1846 and was the property of Gustave Moynier, the first President of the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC). It later hosted the League of Nations in 1926 and served as headquarters for the ICRC from 1933 to 1946. 

image: id: "b28f1bc0-eefc-438a-93d1-2f002bd575b3" file: "b28f1bc0-eefc-438a-93d1-2f002bd575b3" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- The "Maison de la Paix" slug: >- the-maison-de-la-paix ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

The "Maison de la Paix" (or "house of peace" in English), located a few minutes away from the United Nations' Palace of Nations and near most of the International Organizations based in Geneva, is the main building of the Geneva Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies. It provides state-of-the-art facilities for research and teaching, and it is where MIDS students attend a number of their classes as well as public events. It is also where the Graduate Institute's main library is located.

notes: >-

The modern complex, inaugurated in 2013, is considered one of the most remarkable architectural achievements of the 21st century in French-speaking Switzerland, with its iconic glass “petals” and meticulous aesthetics. In addition to the Geneva Graduate Institute, the complex also houses a range of institutions and non-governmental organizations working on issues related to international affairs, diplomacy, and humanitarian action.

image: id: "91dd8d44-dcee-48e2-bcd9-f97d313026f5" file: "91dd8d44-dcee-48e2-bcd9-f97d313026f5" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- Uni Mail slug: >- uni-mail ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

Located in the bustling and student-friendly neighborhood of Plainpalais, it is the main building of the University of Geneva and houses the Faculty of Law, where MIDS students may attend classes from time to time. 

UNIGE's main library as well as several cafeterias can also be found at Uni Mail.

notes: >-

The architectural design of Uni Mail is characterized by great transparency, with large volumes open to the urban environment. A massive complex, it is the largest building in Switzerland dedicated to the humanities. 

image: id: "4828f2b3-7136-441e-bf70-38af9c559e63" file: "4828f2b3-7136-441e-bf70-38af9c559e63" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Libraries slug: >- libraries ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- The Kathryn and Shelby Cullom Davis Library slug: >- the-kathryn-and-shelby-cullom-davis-library ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

Located at the Maison de la Paix, it is the main library of the Geneva Graduate Institute. Specialized in the field of international relations and development studies, this library houses valuable collections that reflect the disciplines taught at the Institute. In addition to its vast repository of resources and e-resources, study rooms and workstations create a perfect academic environment for students.

Learn more about the library's study spaces here - external link. 

notes: >- image: id: "b5317193-c041-47ae-a70e-090a9dddbffe" file: "b5317193-c041-47ae-a70e-090a9dddbffe" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- Uni Mail Library slug: >- uni-mail-library ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

The Uni Mail Library is the largest of several libraries of the University of Geneva located around the city. In addition to vast study spaces, it also allows MIDS students to benefit from a wide array of resources and offerings within the field of international law and beyond.

notes: >- image: id: "e7323406-e6ff-48c3-b13a-89eea686ce80" file: "e7323406-e6ff-48c3-b13a-89eea686ce80" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Student spaces slug: >- student-spaces ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- The Fab slug: >- the-fab ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

Located inside the Maison de la Paix, the "Fab"--short for French "Fabrique de la paix"--is a combination of a student lounge and an innovation lab. With exhibition spaces, as well as lounge, study, and meeting spots, it is meant to be a space for students to study, socialize, and work together towards common initiatives and projects. 

The Fab also hosts a number of events throughout the years organized by the Graduate Institute and its community.

notes: >- image: id: "23295a5b-f476-47cd-8c94-bc45f55add2e" file: "23295a5b-f476-47cd-8c94-bc45f55add2e" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- Picciotto Common Room slug: >- picciotto-common-room ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

The Geneva Graduate Institute's main students' common space, it is located on the ground floor of the Picciotto student residence, a short walk away from the Maison de la Paix. Students will find a fully equipped kitchen, an outdoor seating area, study and lounge spaces with large TVs, books and magazines, as well as copiers & printers. 

The Picciotto Common Room also includes office space for Graduate Institute student associations, and it is where many student-run social events take place throughout the academic year.

notes: >- image: id: "7bf2a857-1ffe-4cbe-bb70-60beb631585c" file: "7bf2a857-1ffe-4cbe-bb70-60beb631585c" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- Maison de la Paix Lounges slug: >- maison-de-la-paix-lounges ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

In addition to the Fab and the Picciotto Common Room, the Maison de la Paix also offers students a number of lounge spaces to sit down, rest, study, or socialize between classes.
