To select the best students, the MIDS has a strict application procedure; please review it carefully and start your application now!

header_text: >- header_cta: display: false label: >- target: "" external: false contents_reading_time: seconds: 263 text: >- 4 minutes contents: - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

Admissions to the MIDS program are based on a comprehensive evaluation of each application. While no minimum GPA is required, the admissions process is highly competitive, with fewer than 20% of applicants admitted.

The MIDS Admissions Committee thoroughly reviews all complete applications, placing particular emphasis on academic performance, the motivation letter, and relevant professional experience. Priority is given to applicants who demonstrate both strong academic achievement and a clear, compelling motivation for joining the program.

Applications for the MIDS 2025-2026 program will be open from Thursday, 31 October 2024 to Sunday, 15 December 2024 at 23:59 CET.

Please read all of the following information carefully before starting an application, as it will help ensure a quick and smooth application process.

Applications must be submitted online via the application form. The link for the application form will be available once applications are open. Applications open every year by the end of October.

Supporting documents need to be uploaded into the online application portal in PDF format. Documents sent by e-mail or regular mail will not be considered.

Please read the instructions provided in the application portal carefully and do not forget to submit your application at the end of the process. 

It is the applicant's responsibility to confirm that all supporting documents are uploaded, and the application is validated before the application deadline. Incomplete applications will not be considered.

APPLICATION FEE

ENROLLMENT DEPOSIT


Waitlisted candidates will be required to confirm their intent to remain on the waitlist. Without confirmation, admission may not be offered if a place becomes available.

Only candidates admitted in the first admissions round are invited to apply for MIDS-managed scholarships. Scholarship applications must be submitted within one week of the admission notification.

Candidates admitted from the waitlist cannot apply for MIDS-managed scholarships. 

Enrolled students will receive timely information and materials necessary to apply for housing, visas, etc. They must also submit official, certified copies of academic records.

The non-refundable application fee of CHF 100 (net of bank charges), must be paid in full by Friday, 20 December 2024 at 23:59 CET or the application will not be reviewed.

Instructions on how to make the payment will be provided after submission of the application.

notes: >-

Payment of the application fee does not mean that an application has been submitted, and it does not guarantee review if it is missing required components. It is the applicant’s responsibility to make sure that their complete application is submitted through the portal on time.

The application fee is non-refundable, even in the case of an erroneous, incomplete (and therefore, not reviewed), withdrawn, or rejected application. It cannot be carried over to a future application cycle, and it will not be credited towards the tuition fees in the case of admission to the program.

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- Enrollment Deposit slug: >- enrollment-deposit ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

Admitted candidates must secure their place in the program by submitting a non-refundable deposit of CHF 700 shortly after receiving their admission notification. This deposit will be credited toward the total tuition fees.

notes: >-

Admitted candidates who accept an offer but do not pay the CHF 700 deposit will not be considered officially enrolled and may forfeit their place once the MIDS begins extending offers to waitlisted candidates in April.

Please note that the non-refundable deposit is valid only for the current application cycle. If a candidate withdraws from the program, the deposit cannot be transferred to a future cycle and will not be credited toward tuition fees in the event of another future admission offer.

All admitted candidates must pay the CHF 700 deposit regardless of whether they receive a scholarship, except those awarded a MIDS-managed scholarship covering the full tuition fees.

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- Deferral Policy slug: >- deferral-policy ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

The MIDS does not permit deferrals. Candidates who choose not to enroll for the 2025-2026 academic year and wish to join in the future must reapply in the following admissions cycle. Previous admission to the program does not guarantee admission in a later cycle; each admission cycle is assessed independently.

All information provided in the application and supporting documents will be treated with the strictest confidentiality and will only be reviewed by the MIDS Admissions Committee (including the MIDS Committee and MIDS Staff), MIDS-managed scholarship donors (in the case of a scholarship application), and the MIDS parent institutions, the Geneva Graduate Institute and the University of Geneva.
