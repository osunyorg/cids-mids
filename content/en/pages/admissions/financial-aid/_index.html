The MIDS offers a limited number of scholarships each year; the number of scholarships and amount awarded vary from year to year.

Coverage ranges from full tuition to partial tuition support, with some scholarships also including partial living expense support.

MIDS-managed scholarships are awarded after a careful assessment of each application, considering both financial need and academic merit. Priority is given to outstanding students who would not otherwise be able to undertake the program.

The MIDS scholarship program is possible thanks to the generous support of its donors, among others:

  • Lévy Kaufmann-Kohler Scholarship - external link: Covers full tuition and partial living expenses
  • LALIVE Scholarship - external link: Covers full tuition and partial living expenses
  • Jan Paulsson Scholarship (supported by Three Crowns LLP - external link): Covers full tuition and partial living expenses
  • MIDS-Young ICCA Scholarship - external link: Covers full tuition
  • Epaminondas Lambadarios Scholarship - external link: Provides partial tuition support
  • George Fessas Family Scholarship: Provides partial tuition support
  • MIDS Alumni Association Scholarship: Provides partial tuition support
notes: >-

The MIDS scholarship program also benefits from the generous support of an anonymous donor who provides multiple partial scholarships. These awards further expand the number of partial scholarships available to admitted students in need of financial support.

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Scholarship Application Procedure slug: >- scholarship-application-procedure ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

Only candidates admitted in the first admissions round are invited to apply for MIDS-managed scholarships. Scholarship applications must be submitted within one week of the admission notification.

The scholarship application file must include the following documents:

  • Scholarship Application Form: This form will be made available to applicants in early February and must be completed and submitted as part of the scholarship application.
  • Personal Statement: A written statement outlining the applicant’s financial need and specifying why a partial or full scholarship is required to undertake the MIDS program.
  • Income Statements: Copies of official statements detailing income from the applicant and/or any other relevant individuals (e.g., family members or other financial supporters) who may be contributing financially.
  • Proof of Available Funds and Assets: An official statement from a bank or other savings institution, and/or tax forms, indicating available funds and assets that can support the applicant’s studies.
  • Other Scholarships or Funding Sources: A list of other scholarships, grants, or funding (including loan applications or employer support) the applicant has pursued, along with copies of any responses received to date.
  • MIDS-Young ICCA Scholarship (if applicable): To apply for the MIDS-Young ICCA scholarship, applicants must submit an additional motivation letter (maximum two pages) to info@mids.ch. Applicants must be Young ICCA members to be eligible for this scholarship.

The MIDS Admissions Committee reviews all scholarship applications. Applications are then forwarded to relevant donors, who make the final decision on awards.

If a scholarship-awarded candidate declines admission, the scholarship may be reassigned to the next eligible first-round admitted candidate.

Waitlisted candidates are not eligible to apply for MIDS-managed scholarships.

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- Confidentiality slug: >- confidentiality ranks: self: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

All information provided in scholarship applications will be treated confidentially and reviewed only by the MIDS Admissions Committee, scholarship donors, and MIDS partner institutions (Geneva Graduate Institute and University of Geneva).

Donors receive only the scholarship form, personal statement, and CV, while income or bank statements remain confidential and are reviewed solely by the MIDS Admissions Committee.
