MIDS-Managed Scholarships

The MIDS offers between 8 and 12 scholarships each year (the number of scholarships and amount awarded vary from year to year). Coverage ranges from full tuition to partial tuition support, with some scholarships also including partial living expense support. MIDS-managed scholarships are awarded after a careful assessment of each application, considering both financial need and academic merit. Priority is given to outstanding students who would not otherwise be able to undertake the programme.

MIDS-Managed Scholarships
Lévy Kaufmann-Kohler Scholarship
Lévy Kaufmann-Kohler  has been a significant partner of the MIDS since the programme's inception in 2008. The Lévy Kaufmann-Kohler Scholarship 
covers full tuition fees and living expenses for one MIDS student each year.

LALIVE Scholarship
LALIVE  has shared a long-standing and impactful relationship with the MIDS, nearly since the programme’s founding. The LALIVE Scholarship covers the
full tuition fees and living expenses for one MIDS student each year.

Three Crowns Jan Paulsson Scholarship
Established in 2021 by  Three Crowns LLP , the Jan Paulsson Scholarship provides support for one MIDS student from a developing economy 
with demonstrated financial need .    The Jan Paulsson Scholarship

covers full tuition and partial living expenses , honoring Jan Paulsson's commitment to mentoring the next generation of arbitration practitioners.

Epaminondas Lambadarios Scholarship
The Epaminondas Lambadarios Scholarship was established in 2020 by the  Lambadarios Law Firm   in memoriam of Epaminondas Lambadarios. The scholarship partially covers tuition fees for one MIDS student who is a graduate of a Greek university. The recipient is selected on the basis of both academic merit and financial need, honoring the legacy of Epaminondas Lambadarios and his dedication to legal education and mentorship.

MIDS Alumni Association Scholarship
The MIDS Alumni Scholarship Fund was established in 2018 to provide  partial tuition support for one MIDS student each year.

The MIDS scholarship programme also benefits from the generous support of additional anonymous donors, who provide further partial tuition scholarships . These contributions expand access to the programme for admitted students with demonstrated financial need.

Scholarship Application Procedure

Only candidates admitted in the first admissions round are invited to apply for MIDS-managed scholarships. Scholarship applications must be submitted within one week of the admission notification. The MIDS Admissions Committee reviews all scholarship applications. Applications are then forwarded to relevant donors, who make the final decision on awards.

The scholarship application file must include the following documents:
Scholarship Application Form
This form will be made available to applicants in early February and must be completed and submitted as part of the scholarship application.

Personal Statement
A written statement outlining the applicant’s financial need and specifying why a partial or full scholarship is required to undertake the MIDS program.

Income Statements
Copies of official statements detailing income from the applicant and/or any other relevant individuals (e.g., family members or other financial supporters) who may be contributing financially.

Proof of Available Funds and Assets
An official statement from a bank or other savings institution, and/or tax forms, indicating available funds and assets that can support the applicant’s studies.

Other Scholarships or Funding Sources
A list of other scholarships, grants, or funding (including loan applications or employer support) the applicant has pursued, along with copies of any responses received to date.

MIDS-Young ICCA Scholarship (if applicable)
To apply for the MIDS-Young ICCA scholarship, applicants must submit an additional motivation letter (maximum two pages) to info@mids.ch . Applicants must be Young ICCA members to be eligible for this scholarship.

Deadline
for candidates awarded scholarships to accept or decline the admission offer

If a scholarship-awarded candidate declines admission, the scholarship may be reassigned to the next eligible 1st Batch admitted candidate. Waitlisted candidates are not eligible to apply for MIDS-managed scholarships.

Confidentiality
All information provided in scholarship applications will be treated confidentially and reviewed only by the MIDS Admissions Committee, scholarship donors, and MIDS partner institutions (Geneva Graduate Institute and University of Geneva). Donors receive only the scholarship form, personal statement, and CV, while income or bank statements remain confidential and are reviewed solely by the MIDS Admissions Committee.

Important note
Visa and residence permit fees are not covered by MIDS-managed scholarships. These administrative costs must be paid by the student directly and are part of the standard requirements for studying in Switzerland.
