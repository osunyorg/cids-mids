When it was created in 2008, the MIDS was the first degree of its kind, established as a bold attempt to create an innovative program catering to the specific needs of the practice of international dispute settlement. Now, its reputation speaks for itself: it's trained more than 500 of the world's best lawyers and serves as a reference in the field.

author: >- Professor Zachary Douglas, KC job: >- Director of the CIDS and the MIDS program photo: id: "6a02119a-1b2d-4689-896b-c7236bacfa4b" file: "6a02119a-1b2d-4689-896b-c7236bacfa4b" - kind: block template: title title: >- What is the MIDS? slug: >- what-is-the-mids ranks: self: 2 data: layout: collapsed - kind: block template: organizations title: >- Our parent institutions slug: >- our-parent-institutions ranks: self: 3 children: 4 data: layout: grid description: >-

The MIDS is a joint academic program of two top and well-known Swiss academic institutions: the University of Geneva and the Geneva Graduate Institute, who jointly award the MIDS degree--recognized as a "Master of advanced studies" in line with Swiss and European regulations. On a day-to-day basis, the MIDS is administered by the Geneva Centre for International Dispute Settlement.
