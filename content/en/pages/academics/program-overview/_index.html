Completing an Internship for Credit

- kind: block template: title title: >- A Simple Overall Structure slug: >- a-simple-overall-structure ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: features title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: 2 children: 3 data: description: >-

The MIDS combines a curriculum common to all students, providing them with key, foundational knowledge, alongside a modular curriculum, allowing students to focus on specific areas they want to specialize in, delivered in a variety of formats throughout the year, catering to students' needs and preferences. The unique strength of the MIDS is also to offer a large number of complementary learning opportunities and to have the goal of professional development built into the program.

As such, more than an ordinary graduate program, the MIDS is more of a comprehensive learning and professionalization journey.

elements: - title: >- Core curriculum description: >-

A combination of more traditional lecture-type as well as skills-based pedagogy, the core curriculum constitutes the academic backbone of the MIDS and is the same for all students.

The core curriculum is composed of: 

- 5 core courses and their tutorials

- 4 core workshops

This other key element of the curriculum lets students tailor their learning experience and deepen their expertise in areas that matter most to them. 

The core curriculum is composed of:

The third pillar of the MIDS, the enrichment curriculum offers a wide range of additional activities meant to broaden students' perspectives, experiences, and skills.  

The enrichment curriculum is composed of:

- An academic retreat

- Short workshops

- Institutional visits

- Conferences

- Seminars and lectures

image: id: "ce389670-f574-4db3-a5d2-fae2e21bf933" file: "ce389670-f574-4db3-a5d2-fae2e21bf933" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- A Week at the MIDS slug: >- a-week-at-the-mids ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: 2 data: layout: no_background text: >-

The MIDS is an intensive program, in every sense. An average week comprises about 20 hours of in-person courses, for which students must thoroughly prepare in advance (approximately two to three hours of preparation per hour of class time).

The MIDS teaching system requires an active classroom, where students are called on to express their opinions, discuss the views expressed by classmates, and comment on required readings.

In the autumn semester, the two core courses take place on a regular schedule of once per week, but the rest of the program components (spring core courses, workshops, tutorials, lectures, seminars, study trips, conferences, etc.) do not follow a regular pattern.

The flexibility of the MIDS schedule allows for quick adaption to the schedules of our in-demand faculty, and the possibility to add in exciting new opportunities at short notice. No two weeks look alike at the MIDS.

The MIDS requires total commitment, and the result is a transformative experience where working hard pays off.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Curriculum Elements at a Glance slug: >- curriculum-elements-at-a-glance ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: definitions title: >- What does the MIDS curriculum consist of? slug: >- what-does-the-mids-curriculum-consist-of ranks: self: 3 data: description: >- elements: - title: >- Core Curriculum description: >-

The core curriculum provides a foundation of essential knowledge and skills that all students must acquire, ensuring a consistent learning experience across the cohort.

- title: >- Modular Curriculum description: >-

The modular curriculum allows students to tailor their learning experience, fostering a personalized academic journey and deepening their expertise in areas that matter most to them. Students select courses that align with their interests, write a thesis on a subject of their choice, and have the option to pursue an internship experience for academic credit.

- title: >- Enrichment Curriculum description: >-

The enrichment curriculum offers a diverse range of supplemental activities designed to broaden students' perspectives and enhance their overall learning experience. This includes a mini-moot court, short workshops, institutional visits, conferences, seminars, and lectures.

- title: >- Core Courses description: >-

Five compulsory courses across both semesters that provide a comprehensive overview of international dispute settlement.

- title: >- Tutorials description: >-

Small group sessions led by MIDS lecturers to revisit and further explore the content of the Core Courses.

- title: >- Core Workshops description: >-

Clinical workshops designed to advance skills required in international dispute settlement practice.

- title: >- Research Papers description: >-

Students actively participate in discussions and present two research papers throughout the autumn semester, engaging with both course content and their classmates' work.

- title: >- Intensive Courses description: >-

Intensive courses, organized by the MIDS and taught by distinguished faculty and professionals, offer in-depth exploration of various topics in international dispute settlement, focusing on specialized mechanisms or specific issues. They typically consist of nine hours of class over two or three days.

- title: >- Elective Courses description: >-

Semester-long weekly courses offered by the University of Geneva or the Geneva Graduate Institute that allow for a deeper engagement in dispute settlement-related areas or exploration of adjacent fields.

- title: >- LL.M. Thesis description: >-

Compulsory scholarly paper addressing topics related to international dispute settlement chosen by students, written under the guidance of a faculty member.

- title: >- Internship Experience description: >-

The MIDS program offers students the opportunity to enhance their education by substituting one elective course with an internship experience, which is counted towards their graduation requirements. Internships may take place at a law firm or institution specializing in international dispute settlement.

- title: >- Academic Retreat description: >-

Two-day event focused on a moot court exercise in international arbitration.

- title: >- Compact Workshops description: >-

Focused, short-format sessions taking place over a few hours, offering valuable learning and skill development. Topics include career services, academic research and writing, and thesis development.

- title: >- Institutional Visits description: >-

Visits to international dispute settlement institutions in Switzerland or abroad (Paris, The Hague).

- title: >- Conferences description: >-

External conferences in Switzerland or abroad organized by professional associations or other institutions; MIDS students attend one or two conferences of their choice, in Switzerland or abroad.

- title: >- Public lectures description: >-

Public events featuring distinguished academics and practitioners to discuss current dispute resolution issues and developments.

- title: >- Seminars description: >-

Closed-door lectures given to MIDS students by prominent guest speakers.
