- kind: block template: title title: >- A Simple Overall Structure slug: >- a-simple-overall-structure ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: features title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: 2 children: 3 data: description: >-

The MIDS combines a curriculum common to all students, providing them with key, foundational knowledge, alongside a modular curriculum, allowing students to focus on specific areas they want to specialize in, delivered in a variety of formats throughout the year, catering to students' needs and preferences. The unique strength of the MIDS is also to offer a large number of complementary learning opportunities and to have the goal of professional development built into the program.

As such, more than an ordinary graduate program, the MIDS is more of a comprehensive learning and professionalization journey.

elements: - title: >- Core curriculum description: >-

A combination of more traditional lecture-type as well as skills-based pedagogy, the core curriculum constitutes the academic backbone of the MIDS and is the same for all students.

The core curriculum is composed of: 

- 5 core courses and their tutorials

- 4 core workshops

image: id: "c4a00c8d-1150-44e8-9e0b-df3e0e5f5a3a" file: "c4a00c8d-1150-44e8-9e0b-df3e0e5f5a3a" alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Modular curriculum description: >-

This other key element of the curriculum lets students tailor their learning experience and deepen their expertise in areas that matter most to them. 

The core curriculum is composed of: 

- 8 intensive courses

- 2 elective courses

- An LL.M. thesis

- An optional internship experience

image: id: "b574fc3d-798f-4469-a534-41b1c9948ada" file: "b574fc3d-798f-4469-a534-41b1c9948ada" alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Enrichment Curriculum description: >-

The third pillar of the MIDS, the enrichment curriculum offers a wide range of additional activities meant to broaden students' perspectives, experiences, and skills.  

The enrichment curriculum is composed of:

- An academic retreat

- Short workshops

- Institutional visits

- Conferences

- Seminars and lectures

image: id: "5f69e805-e84b-400c-bbdc-387dd5685b17" file: "5f69e805-e84b-400c-bbdc-387dd5685b17" alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Career Services description: >-

Last but not least, the career services offered to students throughout the year are considered an integral part, and another key pillar of the MIDS curriculum. 

The career services offer a range of activities including:

- Workshops

- Individual counseling

- Networking opportunities.

image: id: "ce389670-f574-4db3-a5d2-fae2e21bf933" file: "ce389670-f574-4db3-a5d2-fae2e21bf933" alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- An intensive program slug: >- an-intensive-program ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: 2 data: layout: no_background text: >-

The MIDS is an intensive program. An average week comprises about 20 hours of in-class courses for which students have to thoroughly prepare in advance (2 to 3 hours of preparation for hour of class is a good estimate).

The MIDS teaching system requires an active class, where students are called on to express their opinions, discuss the views expressed by classmates and comment on required readings.

Some courses take place every week at the same time and day, but many of the other activities (workshops, lectures, seminars, conferences, study trips, networking events,…) do not follow a regular pattern and they are scattered throughout the program. That is why no two weeks look alike at MIDS.

Our students should be prepared to fully commit themselves. MIDS is a transformative experience where working hard pays off.

- title: >- Intensive courses description: >-

Typically consisting of nine hours of class over two or three days, these courses allow students to revisit topics from the core curriculum and reflect on legal theory or philosophical aspects of dispute settlement.

- title: >- Elective courses description: >-

Semester-long weekly courses offered by the University of Geneva or the Geneva Graduate Institute that allow for a deeper engagement in dispute settlement-related areas or exploration of adjacent fields.

- title: >- LL.M. Thesis description: >-

Compulsory scholarly paper addressing topics related to international dispute settlement chosen by students, written under the guidance of a faculty member.

- title: >- Internship experience description: >-

Opportunity for MIDS students to substitute one elective course with an internship at a law firm or institution specializing in international dispute settlement, and which is counted towards their graduation requirements.

- title: >- Academic retreat description: >-

Two-day event focused on a moot court exercise in international arbitration

- title: >- Compact workshops description: >-

Focused, short-format sessions taking place over a few hours, offering valuable learning and skill development.

- title: >- Institutional visits description: >-

Visits to international dispute settlement institutions in Switzerland or abroad (Paris, The Hague).

- title: >- International conferences description: >-

External conferences in Switzerland or abroad organized by professional associations or other institutions that MIDS students attend once or twice a year.

- title: >- Public lectures description: >-

Public events featuring distinguished academics and practitioners to discuss current dispute resolution issues and developments.

- title: >- Seminars description: >-

Closed-door lectures given to MIDS students by prominent guest speakers.

- title: >- Q&A sessions with Start Arbitrators description: >-

Yearly informal interactive discussions with top arbitrators. 

- title: >- Collaborative seminar series description: >-

Yearly seminars organized in partnership with law firms and arbitral institutions based in Geneva.
