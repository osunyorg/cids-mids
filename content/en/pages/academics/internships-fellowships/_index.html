Internships and fellowships offer an exciting opportunity for MIDS students to enhance their practical skills in the field of international arbitration.

Because we recognize the importance of work experience as part of the MIDS training, our students can utilize internship experiences to count towards their credit requirements.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Internships slug: >- internships ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

Because Geneva offers many opportunities, many of our students complete one or several internships during their time at the MIDS or after graduation.

Students are encouraged to explore internship opportunities throughout the year and the MIDS career services are available to support them in their endeavors and through application processes. 

The MIDS has also established partnerships with law firms and international institutions, open exclusively to MIDS students.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- Lévy Kaufmann-Kohler internship slug: >- levy-kaufmann-kohler-internship ranks: self: 3 data: layout: alt_background text: >-

Lévy Kaufmann-Kohler has been a key partner of the MIDS since the program’s founding in 2008.  The firm recruits one or two interns each academic year to pursue a six month paid internship in Geneva after the completion of the MIDS courses but as part of the program.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: chapter title: >- LALIVE internship slug: >- lalive-internship ranks: self: 3 data: layout: alt_background text: >-

The MIDS and LALIVE have a long-standing partnership, dating back to the beginning of the program. As part of that partnership, LALIVE offers a six-month paid internship in Geneva to approximately five students each academic year, commencing after the completion of MIDS courses.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- PCA Fellowship slug: >- pca-fellowship ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-

The PCA is an intergovernmental organization with 122 Contracting Parties that have acceded to one or both of the PCA’s founding conventions. Established in 1899 to facilitate arbitration and other forms of dispute resolution between States, the PCA has developed into a modern, multifaceted institution meeting the dispute resolution needs of the international community.

By virtue of an agreement between the Permanent Court of Arbitration and the MIDS, one student each year has the opportunity to join the PCA’s Secretariat in The Hague for a period of twelve months.

The MIDS fellow will assist in the PCA's International Bureau after they have completed all their mandatory coursework.

The PCA's Secretariat - the International Bureau - consists of an experienced team of legal and administrative staff of various nationalities. It is headed by its Secretary-General. The bureau provides administrative support to tribunals and commissions, serving as the official channel of communications and ensuring safe custody of documents. The International Bureau provides services such as financial administration, logistical and technical support for meetings and hearings, travel arrangements, and general secretarial and linguistic support. It also provides administrative support to tribunals or commissions conducting PCA dispute settlement proceedings outside The Netherlands.
