title: >- International Law description: >- Set of rules, norms, and principles that govern relations between states, international organizations, and, in some cases, individuals, regulating issues like diplomacy, trade, human rights, and conflict. - title: >- Adjudication description: >- A formal, binding process where an impartial body, such as an international court or tribunal, resolves a dispute based on legal principles. - title: >- Arbitration description: >- A process where disputing parties agree to submit their conflict to an impartial tribunal for a binding decision. - title: >- Mediation description: >- A voluntary process where a neutral third party helps disputing parties reach a mutually acceptable agreement. - title: >- Conciliation description: >- A non-binding dispute resolution process where a conciliator facilitates discussions and proposes solutions. - title: >- Negotiation description: >- A direct dialogue between disputing parties aimed at reaching a settlement without third-party intervention. - title: >- Interstate Disputes description: >- Disputes between sovereign states, often resolved through negotiation, arbitration, or adjudication in international courts like the ICJ. - title: >- Investor-State Dispute description: >- When foreign investors to bring claims against host states for alleged breaches of investment treaties. - title: >- Commercial Disputes description: >- Conflicts between private parties from different countries, often resolved through arbitration or litigation concerning contracts, trade, or business transactions. - title: >- Trade Disputes description: >- Disagreements over international trade rules, tariffs, and regulations, typically handled by the WTO’s dispute settlement system. - title: >- International Courts and Tribunals description: >- Judicial bodies established to resolve disputes between states, international organizations, or individuals under international law. They provide binding or advisory decisions on matters such as human rights, trade, criminal justice, and territorial disputes. - title: >- Arbitral Tribunals description: >- Panels of independent arbitrators appointed to resolve disputes outside of court. They provide binding decisions based on the parties’ agreements, commonly used in international commercial and investment disputes. - title: >- International Dispute Resolution Mechanisms description: >- Processes used to resolve conflicts between states, organizations, or individuals under international law. They include, notably, the International Centre for the Settlement of Investment Disputes (ICSID), the Permanent Court of ARbitration (PCA), the World Trade Organization (WTO) Dispute Settlement Body, and many others. - kind: heading title: >- Research at the CIDS slug: "research-at-the-cids" position: 5 rank: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: no_background text: >-
