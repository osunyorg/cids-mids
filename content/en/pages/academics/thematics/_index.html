Across the core curriculum's different modules, MIDS students learn about the variety and complexity of dispute resolution mechanisms, but also about the key consistent features of international dispute settlement questions that arise across the spectrum of forms dispute resolution can take in practice--whether it is inter-state disputes before international courts or international commercial arbitration etc.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Public International Law description: >-

Although it is not always known, a good international dispute settlement lawyer must have strong foundations in public international law. Throughout the program, and irrespective of what careers they intend to undertake, students therefore strengthen and further their knowledge and understanding of most key and transversal issues of public international law that often arise in practice. 

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- Comparative Law description: >-

Alongside international law as such, the practice of international dispute settlement today requires a solid understanding of what domestic legal frameworks provide. By focusing on the English, French, and Swiss systems, the core curriculum exposes students to different approaches and gives them the tools to understand any other legal system they may encounter in their career.

alt: >- credit: >- - title: >- International Dispute Settlement Bodies and Procedures description: >-

While international disputes can be resolved before a wide range of bodies, private, public, international, regional, or domestic, the MIDS curriculum makes sure that students acquire a robust knowledge and understanding of the role and operation of today's key international bodies, from the International Court of Justice to the Permanent Court of Arbitration, the International Chamber of Commerce, and even the International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea. 

alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Specific Areas of Interest slug: >- specific-areas-of-interest ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: chapter title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: layout: alt_background text: >-

In addition to the foundational knowledge needed for all international dispute settlement lawyers, the unique and elaborate MIDS curriculum gives students many opportunities to specialize in the areas that interest them most, but also to expand their horizons by learning about new issues or new areas and perhaps discover a new passion.

This is the purpose of the modular curriculum, as part of which intensive courses as well as elective courses let students choose among dozens of different subjects. 

Additionally, by selecting a topic of their choice for their LL.M. thesis, and by deciding whether and where to undertake an internship experience, students get a chance to learn by doing and to solidify their expertise.

In the past years, students have been able to gain expertise in some of the areas listed below.

Still confused about what to specialize in or about aspects of what studying international dispute settlement involves? We got you! 
Explore the glossary below to learn more about some of the areas and aspects of international dispute settlement you might study at the MIDS.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: block template: title title: >- Glossary of International Dispute Settlement slug: >- glossary-of-international-dispute-settlement ranks: self: 2 data: layout: classic - kind: block template: definitions title: >- slug: >- ranks: self: false data: description: >- elements: - title: >- International Dispute Settlement description: >-

Process of resolving conflicts between states, international organizations, or private entities through negotiation, arbitration, mediation, or adjudication by international courts or tribunals, based on international law and agreements.

- title: >- International Law description: >-

Set of rules, norms, and principles that govern relations between states, international organizations, and, in some cases, individuals, regulating issues like diplomacy, trade, human rights, and conflict.

- title: >- Adjudication description: >-

A formal, binding process where an impartial body, such as an international court or tribunal, resolves a dispute based on legal principles.

- title: >- Arbitration description: >-

A process where disputing parties agree to submit their conflict to an impartial tribunal for a binding decision.

- title: >- Mediation description: >-

A voluntary process where a neutral third party helps disputing parties reach a mutually acceptable agreement.

- title: >- Conciliation description: >-

A non-binding dispute resolution process where a conciliator facilitates discussions and proposes solutions.

- title: >- Negotiation description: >-

A direct dialogue between disputing parties aimed at reaching a settlement without third-party intervention.

- title: >- Interstate Disputes description: >-

Disputes between sovereign states, often resolved through negotiation, arbitration, or adjudication in international courts like the ICJ.

- title: >- Investor-State Dispute description: >-

When foreign investors to bring claims against host states for alleged breaches of investment treaties.

- title: >- Commercial Disputes description: >-

Conflicts between private parties from different countries, often resolved through arbitration or litigation concerning contracts, trade, or business transactions.

- title: >- Trade Disputes description: >-

Disagreements over international trade rules, tariffs, and regulations, typically handled by the WTO’s dispute settlement system.

- title: >- International Courts and Tribunals description: >-

Judicial bodies established to resolve disputes between states, international organizations, or individuals under international law. They provide binding or advisory decisions on matters such as human rights, trade, criminal justice, and territorial disputes.

- title: >- Arbitral Tribunals description: >-

Panels of independent arbitrators appointed to resolve disputes outside of court. They provide binding decisions based on the parties’ agreements, commonly used in international commercial and investment disputes.

- title: >- International Dispute Resolution Mechanisms description: >-

Processes used to resolve conflicts between states, organizations, or individuals under international law. They include, notably, the International Centre for the Settlement of Investment Disputes (ICSID), the Permanent Court of ARbitration (PCA), the World Trade Organization (WTO) Dispute Settlement Body, and many others.
