Yang obtained her LL.B. degree from Southwest University of Political Science and Law and holds the Chinese Legal Professional Qualification Certificate.

During her undergraduate studies, Yang completed her thesis on “Governance of Digital Trade under the Goods-Services Dichotomy of the WTO”. She has also pursued academic studies at the University of California, Berkeley and Bielefeld University, where she gained an international perspective and enhanced her multilingual proficiency. 

Before joining MIDS, Yang accumulated extensive practical experience in the field of international dispute settlement. She completed a long-term internship at CIETAC, followed by an internship in the Dispute Resolution Division of Fangda Partners. Through her involvement in a wide range of cases, covering construction, joint ventures and commercial transactions, Yang developed a deep understanding of issues related to international commercial arbitration and litigation, as well as re-arbitration proceedings.
