Eric Zadeh is a French-Iranian lawyer. With a PhD in public international law from the Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies, his initial field of specialization is international human rights law.

On an academic level, he has worked as a teaching assistant and ‘chargé de cours’ at the Strasbourg University, the Geneva Graduate Institute and the Global Studies Institute. He has also been a faculty member at many private universities, incuding the Franco-Lebanese University and Tishk International University in Erbil (Iraq) as well as the Webster University in Tashkent.

In addition, he has many years of experience as a consultant to government institutions and international organisms. In particular, he has served as a legal advisor to the Kurdistan Regional Government of Iraq and before joining MIDS, he was serving as a Human Rights Officer at the Office of the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights.

With his interdisciplinary and language skills, his objective is to open up to new professional horizons and to collaborate with law firms and international agencies targeted by MIDS curriculum, in particular those working with or in Gulf countries and in the Middle East in general.
