The MIDS and the MIDS Alumni Association is delighted to welcome you to a special lecture by Mr. Vivekananda Neelakantan, Registrar of the Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC), on:

Date: Thursday 9 October 2025, 18h00

Venue: Salle PHIL 201 at Université de Geneve (bâtiment des Philosophes, 22, blvd des Philosophes)

body: >-

Vivekananda
Neelakantan Registrar,
Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC)

Mr. Vivekananda
Neelakantan serves as Registrar of SIAC, one of the world's leading arbitral
institutions. He oversees the administration of cases and the development of
SIAC's procedural framework, ensuring efficiency, transparency, and integrity
in international dispute resolution. Prior to joining SIAC, Mr. Neelakantan
held senior roles in both private practice and academia, and has extensive
experience in international arbitration, public international law, and
commercial litigation.

He has advised and
represented governments, multinational corporations, and arbitral tribunals
across a range of sectors including infrastructure, energy, and finance. Mr.
Neelakantan regularly speaks on topics such as institutional arbitration,
procedural innovation, and the evolving role of arbitral tribunals in complex
disputes.

This lecture will
offer valuable insights into the work of SIAC and the new SIAC arbitration
rules. This event is designed both for those exploring a career in
arbitration and for established practitioners seeking deeper insights into the SIAC
framework. 

Register

Join us for an
engaging discussion on the SIAC and the SIAC arbitration rules.

Vivekananda Neelakantan Registrar, Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC)

Mr. Vivekananda Neelakantan serves as Registrar of SIAC, one of the world's leading arbitral institutions. He oversees the administration of cases and the development of SIAC's procedural framework, ensuring efficiency, transparency, and integrity in international dispute resolution. Prior to joining SIAC, Mr. Neelakantan held senior roles in both private practice and academia, and has extensive experience in international arbitration, public international law, and commercial litigation. He has advised and represented governments, multinational corporations, and arbitral tribunals across a range of sectors including infrastructure, energy, and finance. Mr. Neelakantan regularly speaks on topics such as institutional arbitration, procedural innovation, and the evolving role of arbitral tribunals in complex disputes. This lecture will offer valuable insights into the work of SIAC and the new SIAC arbitration rules. This event is designed both for those exploring a career in arbitration and for established practitioners seeking deeper insights into the SIAC framework.
