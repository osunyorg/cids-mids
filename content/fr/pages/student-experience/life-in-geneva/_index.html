Located at the heart of Europe, a few minutes away from the French border, and one train-ride away from Italy or Germany, home to 40 international organizations, 750 NGOs, and numerous multinational companies, and with foreign nationals making up 40% of its population, Geneva is an inherently international city. On your commute to class, you may hear any of the languages spoken in the 196 countries represented at the United Nations. No matter where you're from, you can be sure to find a community in Geneva. And if you feel like exploring, you can travel to most European capitals in just a few hours.

Geneva is regularly featured in the top spots of rankings of the world's most liveable cities, while Switzerland is often in the top ten of the world's "happiest" countries. A cosmopolitan city, Geneva stands out in education and research, with an excellent school system and many world-leading higher education programs and research institutions. It is also known as an exceptionally safe and clean city, one with robust infrastructures, effective public transport, and a large number of outdoor public spaces to relax. With hundreds of festivals throughout the year focused on food, music, arts, films, Geneva also boasts a vibrant cultural life. A leisure and sports-friendly city, workplaces in Geneva also often offer a better work-life balance than most other international hubs.
